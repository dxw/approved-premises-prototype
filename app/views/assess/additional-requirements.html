Consider if the probation practitioner should seek assisstance or intervention from other agencies to support effective management in the community, or address risk issues and additional needs. For example:

  • GP support
  • Mental health services
  • Drug and alcohol support
  • Gambling support
  • Womens services
  • Care Act assessment
  • Assisstance from the local authority with care and support needs
  • Liaison with accommodation providers regarding move on plans

There may be occasions where there is evidence that a person’s behaviour would pose an unacceptable risk to staff, self, others in the AP, or the local community which would not be mitigated by the proposed licence conditions or the regime and security of the AP.

In these instances, the assessor cannot decide the application is unsuitable without first discussing the application with an APAM to identify potential mitigations to make the risk manageable. This may include the need for additional resources.
